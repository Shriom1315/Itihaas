THE STORY OF
MAHARANA
PRATAP
Maharana Pratap was the 13th king of Mewar, a region in north-western India in the present-day state of Rajasthan. Maharana Pratap is known for leading the Rajput resistance against the expansionist policy of the Mughal Emperor Akbar, including the Battle of Haldighati and Battle of Dewair, which have turned him into a folk hero.
THE STORY OF
CHANDRAGUPT
MAURYA
Chandragupta founder of the Maurya dynasty and the first emperor to unify most of India under one administration. He is credited with saving the country from maladministration and freeing it from foreign domination. He later fasted to death in sorrow for his famine-stricken people.
THE STORY OF
SHIVAJI
MAHARAJ
Shivaji Maharaj, born in 1630, was a visionary 17th-century Maratha king who founded the Maratha Empire in western India. Known for his military prowess, he championed guerrilla warfare, established a navy, and became the Chhatrapati in 1674. Beyond his military achievements, Shivaji was a skilled statesman, implementing inclusive governance principles and fostering economic development. His legacy endures through the enduring impact on Indian history, with forts and monuments standing testament to his leadership, valor, and commitment to a just and inclusive rule.
THE STORY OF
GURU GOVIND
SINGH
Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Sikh Guru, shaped Sikhism through his spiritual leadership and courageous stance against Mughal persecution. In 1699, he established the Khalsa, solidifying Sikh identity. A warrior, poet, and philosopher, Guru Gobind Singh's teachings emphasized equality, bravery, and devotion. His enduring legacy inspires Sikhs globally, symbolizing resilience and strength.
